# Risk Memo — Crestview Union High, 2026 Certificated Layoff (Ed. Code §§ 44949/44955)

**Assigned ALJ:** Lew. The retrieved tendency block reports a respondent-win rate of 0.261 across 23 of this ALJ's decided holdings, which is **not distinguishable from the corpus base rate of 0.24** — so there is **no outcome lean to report** for this ALJ, and nothing below should be read as "this ALJ favors districts/respondents." What the footprint does show is that this ALJ has handled bumping, skipping, and competency disputes at above-corpus frequency (i.e., these issues recur in front of this ALJ), which is relevant to how carefully each must be papered, not to who wins.

## Overall risk summary

The district's sharpest exposure is the **industrial-arts/ROP bumping dispute**: the senior plastics teacher's "general competence" theory runs directly into a fact pattern this ALJ has already decided against a senior teacher (Fortuna Union High (Lew), 2004, 2004030657:1), but the corpus also contains squarely opposed outcomes where a senior teacher *was* allowed to bump into an ROP/vocational position (Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010, g3075), so the result turns entirely on the district's competency-criteria record, not on the law being settled in the district's favor. The **EL-counseling skip** is moderately defensible on the bilingual-need theory but is undercut by a counselor-specific holding that senior counselors with the same PPS credential were entitled to retain counseling positions (Hesperia Unified School District (Abler), 2010, g2638). The **lottery tie-break** is the district's strongest position: every retrieved tie-break holding that reached a result upheld a lottery used as a *final* resort after objective criteria, including before this ALJ (Mother Lode (Lew), 2010, g2959). The **PE/driver-training bump** is weak for the district only if the district can show the junior teacher is in fact retained for driver training; the controlling analog rejected exactly such a bump as speculative (Santa Clara (Cohn), 1983, g837). PKS-validity and attrition issues are low-risk on the retrieved record but the attrition holdings are conflicting enough that the district must lock down its attrition accounting.

## Skipping — EL-counseling caseload skip (retained junior counselor with PPS + bilingual)

**Exposure.** The district proposes to skip a junior school counselor on the strength of a PPS credential plus bilingual capacity, to serve a designated English-learner counseling caseload, with the need recited in the board resolution. This is a "special training and experience" skip under § 44955(d). The evidence here is mixed and the counselor-specific subset is thin.

**What respondents will likely argue.** Drawing on the analogous respondent arguments: that more senior counselors hold the *same* PPS credential and bilingual/CLAD-type authorization and therefore should be retained over the junior employee (the credential-parity argument made in Jurupa Unified (Hewitt), 2009, 2009020840:2, and Paso Robles Unified (Reyes), 2009, 2009031367:0); and the counselor-specific version — that retention should follow seniority among PPS-credentialed counselors regardless of relative counseling experience (Hesperia Unified School District (Abler), 2010, g2638).

**How it has tended to land.** Of the seven retrieved skipping holdings, four have a recorded prevailing party: districts prevailed in three (Jurupa Unified (Hewitt), 2009, 2009020840:2; Lancaster (Montoya), 2009, 2009031291:0; Paso Robles Unified (Reyes), 2009, 2009031367:0), and a respondent prevailed in one (Biggs Unified (Sarli), 2004, 2004040090:1). Three retrieved holdings carry **no recorded prevailing party** (Hesperia Unified School District (Abler), 2010, g2638; Desert Sands Unified School District (Ahler), 2010, g2609; Acalanes (Anderson), 2008, g1705), so they cannot be counted as wins or losses — treat the win/loss tally as 3–1 on four data points, which is **thin**. The pattern in the district-win cases: skips were upheld where the district proved the junior employee had *specific training/experience the senior employees lacked and failed to rebut* (EL literacy-coach training in Lancaster (Montoya), 2009, 2009031291:0; bilingual CLAD certificates tied to a demonstrated EL population in Paso Robles Unified (Reyes), 2009, 2009031367:0; specialized Dual Immersion skill sets in Jurupa Unified (Hewitt), 2009, 2009020840:2). The district lost where it could not show a *specific need* to retain the junior employee and a senior employee was competent (Biggs Unified (Sarli), 2004, 2004040090:1). Two counselor-flavored holdings cut in opposite directions and neither has a recorded outcome: one suggests a Spanish-speaking-counselor need can justify the skip (Desert Sands Unified School District (Ahler), 2010, g2609), the other that senior PPS counselors keep counseling positions despite less experience (Hesperia Unified School District (Abler), 2010, g2638).

**What to shore up.** Build the record that the *junior* counselor has EL-counseling-specific training/experience that senior counselors do not — mirror the literacy-coach/CLAD-evidence approach that carried Lancaster (Montoya), 2009, 2009031291:0 and Paso Robles Unified (Reyes), 2009, 2009031367:0 — rather than relying on credential possession alone, because possession of the PPS credential is exactly what the Hesperia respondents (g2638) used. Document the *specific* EL-counseling need and that no senior counselor can serve it; the loss in Biggs (Sarli), 2004, 2004040090:1 was a failure-of-specific-need case.

## Competency — bumping into counseling, industrial-arts, and PE positions

**Exposure.** Competency is the hinge for all three of the district's contested moves: whether senior counselors can bump the retained EL counselor, whether the senior plastics teacher is competent for the *whole* general-IA/ROP assignment, and whether the senior PE teacher is competent for the junior's assignment. The district's leverage is its board-adopted competency criteria; the risk is an ALJ finding those criteria unreasonable or an "insurmountable barrier."

**What respondents will likely argue.** From the analogous respondent arguments: that an identical credential (here, PPS for counselors; single-subject for PE) authorizes the service and therefore establishes competency to bump (the "credential authorizes, so I'm competent" argument rejected in Los Angeles Unified (Cabos-Owen), 2010, g2936 and made by the classroom teachers in Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:1); that the district's experience-based competency requirement is unreasonable and undercuts seniority (the winning argument in Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:0, where respondents said they were certificated and could learn new standards); and that a competency criterion not properly reduced to writing or referenced in the resolution should not bind (the writing-defect raised, but rejected, in Twin Rivers (Lew), 2009, g2064).

**How it has tended to land.** Seven retrieved holdings. Three have a recorded prevailing party: districts in two (Travis Unified (Tompkin), 2009, 2009020656:2; Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:1), respondent in one (Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:0) — and note both Rocklin outcomes come from the *same* decision, splitting on a per-position basis. Four holdings carry **no recorded prevailing party** (Lemon Grove (Hewitt), 2009, 2009020877:3 — listed as district-prevailing in the record; Twin Rivers (Lew), 2009, g2064; Los Angeles Unified (Cabos-Owen), 2010, g2936; Compton Unified (Shrenger), 2015, g3908). The usable signal: competency criteria are upheld when they are reasonable and the respondent in fact lacks the required recent experience (one-year-in-program rule upheld in Travis Unified (Tompkin), 2009, 2009020656:2; full-credential requirement upheld in Red Bluff (Kopec), 2009, 2009031356:2; ELD special-training skip upheld in Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:1). They fail when framed as a "comparable setting" barrier that effectively defeats a credentialed senior teacher's bump (Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:0). Two outcome-less holdings are directly on point for counselors: the PPS-credential-does-not-equal-competency holding (Los Angeles Unified (Cabos-Owen), 2010, g2936) helps the district's counselor skip, and the visual-arts/performing-arts mismatch holding (Compton Unified (Shrenger), 2015, g3908) helps the district resist a too-broad bump theory by a teacher whose subject overlaps only partially. Of particular note, this ALJ's own Twin Rivers (Lew), 2009, g2064 holding accepted a competency standard *even though it was not referenced in the board resolution or reduced to writing* — but that holding has **no recorded prevailing party**, so it is persuasive context, not a counted win.

**What to shore up.** Frame the counselor and PE competency requirements as *reasonable experience criteria* (recent in-role experience), not as categorical setting barriers — the line between Travis Unified (Tompkin), 2009, 2009020656:2 (upheld) and Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:0 (struck) is exactly reasonable-experience-requirement versus insurmountable-setting-barrier. For the PE bump specifically, do not rely on a comparable-setting theory. Reduce the competency criteria to writing and tie them to the resolution where possible; while Twin Rivers (Lew), 2009, g2064 tolerated the absence of writing, that is a thin reed and the safer record matches the writing requirement.

## Bumping — plastics teacher into general IA/ROP; PE teacher into driver-training position

**Exposure.** This is the district's highest-risk issue. The plastics teacher claims general competence over the *entire* general-industrial-arts/ROP assignment of a junior teacher; the district says he is not competent to teach the full range. The PE teacher claims competence to bump a junior teacher said to be retained for driver training; the district says there is no showing the junior is retained specifically for driver training (the claim is speculative).

**What respondents will likely argue.** The plastics teacher will argue — exactly as the senior respondent did in the closest analog — that the junior employee is retained to teach classes he is "credentialed and competent to teach," with no relevant change in his credentials (Fortuna Union High (Lew), 2004, 2004030657:1). He will lean on the precedent that a senior ROP/vocational teacher with the right designated-subjects credential may bump a junior ROP teacher even where the junior reshaped the curriculum (Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010, g3075). The PE teacher will argue he is credentialed and competent to bump the junior driver-training teacher — the same posture as the senior driver-training teacher in Santa Clara (Cohn), 1983, g837.

**How it has tended to land.** Seven retrieved holdings; two structured ones have a recorded prevailing party and **both went to the district** (Fortuna Union High (Lew), 2004, 2004030657:1; Red Bluff (Kopec), 2009, 2009031356:2). The other five carry **no recorded prevailing party** (Santa Clara (Cohn), 1983, g837; Peralta Community College (Sarkisian), 1981, g531; Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010, g3075; Denair (Wong), 2012, g838 listed as g3858; Tulare County Superintendent of Schools (Levy), 1983, g838). This is **a thin and conflicting record** — do not over-read the two district wins. On the merits the corpus is split for the plastics fact pattern: it contains a holding **directly on the plastics facts** denying a bump because the senior IA teacher whose plastics courses were eliminated could teach *some* but not *all* of the junior's IA services (Peralta Community College (Sarkisian), 1981, g531), and an on-point district win where a senior IA teacher could not teach a *combined* IA-and-art assignment (Fortuna Union High (Lew), 2004, 2004030657:1) — but it *also* contains a holding letting a senior vocational/ROP teacher bump a junior ROP teacher (Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010, g3075), and the governing principle that a senior teacher cannot bump unless competent in *all* of the junior's services, and the district need not split the position to facilitate the bump (Peralta (Sarkisian), 1981, g531; the no-split rule also appears in Tulare County Superintendent of Schools (Levy), 1983, g838 and Biggs Unified (Sarli), 2004, 2004040090:1). For the PE/driver-training bump, the controlling analog rejected the bump: a senior teacher competent in driver training could **not** bump a junior who could be retained for *either* PE *or* driver training where there was **no evidence** the retention was for driver training — the senior teacher's claim was "speculative, being based on a principal's tentative schedule" (Santa Clara (Cohn), 1983, g837), reinforced by the rule that a driver-education teacher cannot bump one competent in *both* driver education and driver training (Tulare County Superintendent of Schools (Levy), 1983, g838).

**What to shore up.** For the plastics dispute, the district must prove the senior teacher is **not competent in the full general-IA/ROP range** the junior actually teaches — the "competent in entirety" requirement of Peralta (Sarkisian), 1981, g531 is the controlling frame, and the district win in Fortuna (Lew), 2004, 2004030657:1 came from a documented competence gap (the art/combined portion). Identify precisely which ROP or specialized component of the junior's assignment the senior teacher lacks, and whether it requires a distinct designated-subjects credential — because Denair (Wong), 2012 (g3858) and Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010 (g3075) both turned on whether the junior was retained for a *credential-specific* course the senior lacked. If the senior holds the same designated-subjects vocational authorization, Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010, g3075 is a serious adverse analog. For the PE bump, the entire issue is evidentiary: the district's "speculative" position holds **only if** it can show the junior PE teacher is *not* specifically retained for driver training — the bump was defeated on exactly that evidentiary gap in Santa Clara (Cohn), 1983, g837. Pin down what the junior is actually retained to teach.

## Tie-breaking — lottery as final tie-breaker

**Exposure.** Two teachers shared the same first date of paid service; after the district applied credential/authorization/units criteria they remained tied, and the district broke the final tie by lottery. The resolution authorizes a lottery only after the objective criteria are exhausted. The attorney's specific worry — should some further criterion have come first — maps cleanly onto the retrieved holdings, and this is the district's **strongest** issue.

**What respondents will likely argue.** From the analogous respondent arguments: that a lottery is arbitrary and violates § 44955(b)'s "needs of the district and students" requirement (Manhattan Beach Unified (Roohk), 2004, 2004030554:0); that *additional* objective criteria (e.g., years of experience) should have been applied before resorting to lot (Mother Lode (Lew), 2010, g2959); that the respondent deserved more credit under a specific tie-break criterion (San Mateo Union High (Astle), 2009, 2009030372:1); or that the lottery was conducted in the wrong sequence relative to other criteria (Marysville Joint Unified (Frink), 2009, 2009030335:0).

**How it has tended to land.** This is the **cleanest** set in the pack. Of seven retrieved holdings, five have a recorded prevailing party and **all five went to the district** (Rowland Unified (Montoya), 2009, 2009030579:4; Temple City Unified (Flores), 2009, 2009030292:2; San Mateo Union High (Astle), 2009, 2009030372:1; Marysville Joint Unified (Frink), 2009, 2009030335:0; Manhattan Beach Unified (Roohk), 2004, 2004030554:0); the other two carry **no recorded prevailing party** but state the same rule favorably (Mother Lode (Lew), 2010, g2959; Golden Plains Unified (Westmore), 2010, g2965). The consistent holding: a lottery is a permissible **final** tie-breaker *after* objective criteria are exhausted, provided the resolution authorizes it (Rowland Unified (Montoya), 2009, 2009030579:4; Manhattan Beach Unified (Roohk), 2004, 2004030554:0). Directly answering the attorney's concern, this ALJ's own Mother Lode (Lew), 2010, g2959 holding states it is **permissible to use a lottery as a final tie-breaker after applying other objective criteria** and rejected the argument that the district had to consider further criteria such as years of experience — though that holding has no recorded prevailing party, so it is strong persuasive context rather than a counted win.

**What to shore up.** Confirm the PKS resolution's text in fact authorizes a lottery *only after* the objective criteria are exhausted (the authorization-in-resolution requirement of Rowland Unified (Montoya), 2009, 2009030579:4) — the matter file indicates it does. Document that the district actually applied the credential/authorization/units criteria first and that the two teachers genuinely remained tied, mirroring the "extensive empirical criteria" record that carried Manhattan Beach Unified (Roohk), 2004, 2004030554:0. Keep a clean record of the lottery procedure and sequence; the only respondent traction in this set was a sequencing challenge, and even that lost (Marysville Joint Unified (Frink), 2009, 2009030335:0).

## PKS validity — reduction of particular kinds of services plus ADA decline

**Exposure.** The basis is a PKS reduction (eliminating selected elective/vocational courses) plus an ADA decline. The retrieved holdings for this issue are **noisy** — several are about temporary/categorically-funded employees rather than PKS-validity proper, and one is a duplicate of the bumping/competency holdings — so the directly-on-point support is thinner than the count of seven suggests.

**What respondents will likely argue.** From the analogous arguments: that inconsistencies between the budget and the PKS resolution show no real intent to reduce the services (Pomona Unified (Nafarrete) / West Contra Costa Unified (Tompkin), 2004, 2004030479:5); or, for any temporary/categorically-funded respondents, that they are entitled to probationary-style layoff protections (Tustin Unified (Juárez), 2009, 2009020907:0; Irvine Unified (Juárez), 2009, 2009030745:0).

**How it has tended to land.** Of the seven retrieved holdings, the most on-point PKS-validity holding went to the district: budget/resolution inconsistencies did **not** invalidate the reduction because the PKS resolution governs (Pomona Unified (Nafarrete), 2004, 2004030479:5). On the categorically-funded/temporary-employee sub-issue the retrieved outcomes **split** — a respondent prevailed to the extent of being allowed to participate in the hearing (Tustin Unified (Juárez), 2009, 2009020907:0), while the district prevailed in treating such employees as temporary without § 44949/44955 protections (Irvine Unified (Juárez), 2009, 2009030745:0). Two holdings have no recorded prevailing party (El Monte (Flores), 2008, g1585; Long Beach Unified School District (Nafarrete), 2011, g3213), and two of the seven (Red Bluff (Kopec), 2009, 2009031356:2; Fortuna Union High (Lew), 2004, 2004030657:1) are bumping/competency holdings duplicated into this bucket and tell us nothing about PKS validity. Net: the genuinely on-point PKS-validity support is **a single district win** (Pomona Unified (Nafarrete), 2004, 2004030479:5) — treat as thin.

**What to shore up.** Make sure the PKS resolution itself cleanly identifies the services being reduced and is internally consistent with the budget — and recognize that even if it is not, the resolution governs (Pomona Unified (Nafarrete), 2004, 2004030479:5). If any noticed employees are temporary or in categorically-funded programs, decide deliberately how to notice them; the Tustin/Irvine split (2009020907:0 / 2009030745:0) shows the characterization is contested and outcome-determinative.

## Attrition — accounting for resignations/retirements against the ADA reduction

**Exposure.** Because the basis includes an ADA decline, the district must account correctly for attrition; an error in the attrition count is a classic route to a rescinded notice. The retrieved holdings present a genuine **conflict** on whether post-resolution attrition must be counted.

**What respondents will likely argue.** From the analogous arguments: that positively-assured attrition (e.g., a 3.0 FTE of resignations) should reduce the ADA-driven layoff count and save them (Oroville City Elementary (Frink), 2004, 2004040004:0); that resignations occurring *after* the resolution but before notice should reduce the notices issued (Benicia Unified (Engeman), 2004, 2004020574:1); that a pending (not-yet-accepted) resignation should be counted before final notices (La Honda-Pescadero (Rasmussen), 2009, 2009040184:3); that a *potential* future retirement should let them claim a position (Willows Unified (Sarli), 2009, 2009030253:3); or an equitable-estoppel theory that the district should have guided them on accruing tie-break points (Fortuna (Johnson), 2009, g2253; Eureka (Johnson), 2009, g2252).

**How it has tended to land.** Seven retrieved holdings. Where a prevailing party is recorded, the **district prevailed** in the contested attrition disputes (Oroville City Elementary (Frink), 2004, 2004040004:0; Benicia Unified (Engeman), 2004, 2004020574:1; Willows Unified (Sarli), 2009, 2009030253:3), and one is expressly **not ruled** (La Honda-Pescadero (Rasmussen), 2009, 2009040184:3). But the *rule* conflicts: Benicia Unified (Engeman), 2004, 2004020574:1 holds positively-assured attrition need **not** be considered in PKS reductions and post-resolution resignations need not change notices, while La Honda-Pescadero (Rasmussen), 2009, 2009040184:3 says a pending resignation **should** be considered before final notices (without ruling), and Oroville (Frink), 2004, 2004040004:0 shows a district that *did* track attrition through several resignations and prevailed because the saved position went to a more-senior, non-respondent teacher. The estoppel theory failed in both Fortuna (Johnson), 2009, g2253 and Eureka (Johnson), 2009, g2252 (no recorded prevailing party, but both reasoned against the respondent) because tie-break criteria were public. Speculative future retirements do not count (Willows Unified (Sarli), 2009, 2009030253:3).

**What to shore up.** Track and document **positively-assured** attrition through the date of final notices, the way the prevailing district did in Oroville (Frink), 2004, 2004040004:0; do not rely on the Benicia (Engeman), 2004, 2004020574:1 "need not consider post-resolution attrition" rule as a safe harbor, because La Honda-Pescadero (Rasmussen), 2009, 2009040184:3 pulls the other way and the cleaner record is to count it. Do not build the case on any teacher's speculative future retirement (Willows Unified (Sarli), 2009, 2009030253:3).

## What to verify

These are legal characterizations and thin spots a human attorney must independently confirm — the known failure mode is a confident rule statement sitting on a correct citation.

- **No ALJ outcome lean.** I have *not* asserted that ALJ Lew leans toward districts or respondents, because the tendency block expressly says the respondent-win rate (0.261) is **not distinguishable from the corpus base (0.24)**. The category "footprint/lift" numbers reflect how *often* this ALJ hears bumping/skipping/competency, not who wins. If anyone reads a win-lean into this memo, that is an error to correct.

- **"Competent in the entirety" / no-duty-to-split rule (bumping).** I have characterized the controlling principle as: a senior employee may bump only if competent to perform **all** of the junior's services, and the district need not split a position to enable the bump. That characterization rests on Peralta Community College (Sarkisian), 1981, g531, Tulare County Superintendent of Schools (Levy), 1983, g838, and Biggs Unified (Sarli), 2004, 2004040090:1. Confirm the rule and, critically, confirm it has **not** shifted given Sacramento COE (Lew), 2010, g3075, which allowed an ROP bump and pulls the other way — the corpus is split and a human must reconcile these before advising the plastics teacher cannot bump.

- **PE/driver-training "speculative" theory.** I rely on Santa Clara (Cohn), 1983, g837 for the proposition that a senior teacher cannot bump a junior who *could* be retained for either of two functions absent evidence the retention is for the bumpable function. This is a **1983** holding with no recorded prevailing party; verify it is still good law and that the matter's facts (what the junior PE teacher is actually retained to teach) truly fit the "speculative" frame before pressing it.

- **Lottery as final tie-breaker under § 44955(b).** I characterize the rule as: a lottery is permissible as a **final** tie-breaker after objective criteria are exhausted, if the resolution authorizes it, and the district need not apply further criteria first. This rests on Mother Lode (Lew), 2010, g2959, Rowland Unified (Montoya), 2009, 2009030579:4, and Manhattan Beach Unified (Roohk), 2004, 2004030554:0. The supporting Lew holding (g2959) has **no recorded prevailing party**; confirm the § 44955(b) "needs of the district" framing and that the resolution language matches.

- **Skip vs. competency framing for counselors.** I treat the EL-counseling skip as governed by the § 44955(d) special-training-and-experience exception and distinguish "PPS credential authorizes but does not establish competency" (Los Angeles Unified (Cabos-Owen), 2010, g2936) from the seniority-among-PPS-counselors result (Hesperia Unified School District (Abler), 2010, g2638). Both are **gold holdings with no recorded prevailing party** and the two cut against each other for counselors specifically — verify which line governs the district's facts.

- **"Comparable setting" barrier vs. reasonable experience criterion (competency).** The line I drew between an unreasonable "comparable setting" barrier (struck in Rocklin Unified (Woollard), 2009, 2009030849:0) and a reasonable recent-experience requirement (upheld in Travis Unified (Tompkin), 2009, 2009020656:2) is the load-bearing distinction for the PE and counselor competency criteria. Confirm the district's actual criteria fall on the "reasonable experience" side.

- **Competency standard need not be in the resolution/in writing.** Twin Rivers (Lew), 2009, g2064 is cited for the proposition that a competency standard can be valid even if not referenced in the board resolution or reduced to writing. This is a **gold holding with no recorded prevailing party** and is a risky reed to lean on — verify before relying on any unwritten criterion; the safer course is to put the criteria in writing.

- **Attrition: must post-resolution attrition be counted?** The retrieved holdings **conflict** (Benicia (Engeman), 2004, 2004020574:1 says no; La Honda-Pescadero (Rasmussen), 2009, 2009040184:3 says it should be considered, without ruling). A human must determine the controlling rule for this district/year before the attrition count is finalized.

- **Outcome-counting caveat.** Several retrieved holdings (all "gold" entries and the "none_ruled" entry) carry **no recorded prevailing party**; I have excluded them from every win/loss tally and labeled the thin issues (PKS-validity, bumping, counselor-skipping) as thin. Verify the underlying decisions before any count is represented to the board as a win rate.
